01
April – Foundations
HOW ENERGY
ACTUALLY WORKS

Build the bedrock. By end of month 1 you should be able to explain every energy source, how it's produced, what it costs, and why the world still runs on fossil fuels despite everything.

🎯 Goal: You can explain the global energy system to someone at a dinner party with confidence.
Week 1 · April
The Global Energy System — The Big Picture
Primary vs. final energy — what the difference means
The global energy mix: oil 34%, coal 27%, gas 25%, rest ~14%
Why 86% of energy is still fossil fuel despite all the headlines
Energy units: kWh, MWh, GJ, EJ, MMBtu, barrels — learn to convert
Energy intensity: why rich nations use more, how efficiency works
The IEA, OPEC, IRENA — what each institution does
Read: IEA World Energy Outlook executive summary (free online)
Resources
IEA World Energy Outlook 2024 — Executive Summary (free)
Energy Institute Statistical Review 2025 (download free)
Our World in Data — Energy section (free, all charts)
MacKay: "Sustainable Energy Without the Hot Air" — Chapters 1–5
📝 Write a 300-word memo: "Why do we still depend on fossil fuels in 2025?"
Week 2 · April
Fossil Fuels in Depth — Coal, Oil & Gas
Coal: types (lignite → anthracite), how it's burned, who uses it
Oil: exploration → drilling → refining → products (petrol, jet fuel, plastics)
Unconventional oil: deep sea, oil sands, shale/tight oil (fracking)
Natural gas: pipelines vs LNG — how it's liquefied, shipped, regasified
The concept of "reserve life" — how many years of supply exist
Carbon intensity: CO₂ per kWh for each fuel type
Why natural gas is called the "bridge fuel" — and why critics disagree
Resources
Yergin, "The Prize" — Part 1 (origins of oil)
EIA: How oil and gas is formed (free explainers)
YouTube: "How does an LNG tanker work?" (multiple good videos)
Bloomberg Quicktake: The shale revolution explained
📝 Memo: "Explain the oil refining chain from crude to consumer in your own words."
Week 3 · April
Nuclear Energy — Everything About the Atom
Fission vs fusion — why fusion is still 20 years away (but less so now)
PWR, BWR, CANDU — different reactor designs explained
The nuclear fuel cycle: uranium mining → enrichment → pellets → spent fuel
Three Mile Island, Chernobyl, Fukushima — what went wrong and why
Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) — the nuclear renaissance thesis
Nuclear vs public perception — why safety statistics are often misread
France's nuclear model vs Germany's Energiewende — compare both cases
Resources
World Nuclear Association — free explainers on all reactor types
Gates, "How to Avoid a Climate Disaster" — nuclear chapter
Podcast: "Decouple" — pro-nuclear perspectives
NuScale, TerraPower websites — read their SMR specs
📝 Memo: "Make the case for and against nuclear as Europe's future baseload."
Week 4 · April
Renewables & the Grid — The Modern Energy System
Solar PV: how it works, efficiency limits, the 90% cost drop since 2010
Wind: onshore vs offshore, capacity factors, why Germany chose wind
Hydropower: dam mechanics, pumped storage, why Brazil is 65% hydro
The intermittency problem — why you can't just "build more solar"
The electricity grid: frequency, balancing, merit order pricing
Negative electricity prices — when and why they happen
LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy) — how to read and use this metric
Batteries and storage: where we are and what 4-hour vs 12-hour means
Resources
Lazard LCOE+ 2025 report (free download)
Ember Energy: European Electricity Review 2025 (free)
MacKay: Chapters 10–18 (wind, solar, the maths)
Podcast: "Volts" by David Roberts — grid episodes
📝 Memo: "Why is Germany's electricity more expensive than France's, despite both being energy transition leaders?"
02
May – Markets
MONEY, MARKETS
& TRADE

Learn how energy is priced, traded, and financed. This is where energy meets economics and finance — your home turf from your management degree.

🎯 Goal: Read an energy market report and understand every data point. Know what moves prices.
Week 5 · May
Oil Markets — Pricing, Benchmarks & OPEC
Brent vs WTI vs Dubai — why three benchmarks exist and their differences
How oil futures work: spot, forward curves, contango vs backwardation
OPEC+: who is in it, how decisions are made, Saudi Arabia's role
Fiscal breakeven prices: Saudi ~$70, Russia ~$65, US shale ~$45
The petrodollar system — why oil must be bought in USD and what that means
Big oil company structure: IOCs vs NOCs (Aramco, BP, Shell, ExxonMobil)
Oil tanker shipping: Very Large Crude Carriers, Strait of Hormuz chokepoint
Resources
Yergin, "The New Map" — oil market chapters
EIA Short-Term Energy Outlook (free monthly report)
S&P Global Platts — oil price explainers (website)
Podcast: "The Energy Gang" — oil market episodes
📝 Memo: "What would cause oil to hit $120/barrel again? List 5 scenarios."
Week 6 · May
Natural Gas Markets & the LNG Revolution
Why gas has 3 separate markets (US/Henry Hub, Europe/TTF, Asia/JKM)
LNG economics: liquefaction plants, tankers, regasification terminals
Pipeline politics: Nord Stream, TurkStream, Yamal — how infrastructure = power
How the 2022 European gas crisis unfolded — week by week logic
How the EU replaced Russian gas: diversification strategy
US LNG exports: why it matters for Europe and global price convergence
Gas storage: seasonal dynamics, "filling" for winter, the EU storage rules
Resources
Zuckerman, "The Frackers" — shale gas chapters
Gas Infrastructure Europe (GIE) — live EU storage tracker
Reuters: "How Europe replaced Russian gas" (series)
IEA: Gas Market Report Q1 2025 (free)
📝 Memo: "Why does European gas cost 3x more than US gas, and can this gap ever close?"
Week 7 · May
Electricity Markets — Power Economics
Wholesale vs retail electricity — how prices work at each stage
The merit order: cheapest generation first, last plant sets the price
Day-ahead vs intraday vs balancing markets
Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) — the tool for financing renewables
Capacity markets: why you need to pay for power plants that rarely run
Contracts for Difference (CfDs): how governments guarantee renewable prices
Why the EU reformed its electricity market design in 2024
Resources
Ember Energy: EU Electricity Review (free, detailed)
EU Council: "How EU electricity is produced and sold" explainer
Aurora Energy Research blog posts (some free)
YouTube: "How electricity markets work" — Euan Mearns channel
📝 Memo: "Explain why building more solar can actually raise average electricity prices in the short term."
Week 8 · May
Energy Finance, Investment & Carbon Markets
How energy projects are financed: project finance, equity, bonds
The EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) — how carbon pricing works
The IRA (Inflation Reduction Act): $370bn in clean energy incentives

Bloomberg NEF and Wood Mackenzie: understand what these firms do
Resources
